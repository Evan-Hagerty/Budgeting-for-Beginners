Intro to Budgeting:

When beginning a budget it can seem like there are a lot of options to choose from. We will be using a very simple 50/30/20 budgeting model. This is when you take 50% of your income for necessities, which would be recurring bills like rent and utilities. You will allocate 30% of your income for wants or everyday expenses like travel and entertainment. Lastly you will allocate 20% of your income for savings and paying off debt.

Five Simple Steps to Budgeting

    1.Figure out your after tax income

This can commonly be found on your pay stub. If you are having automatic 401k or retirement payment taken out, then add them back to get a real income. Also add in other income from side jobs or gigs.

    2.Choose a budgeting plan

Make sure to choose a budget that incorpates all your needs and wants. We will be using the simple 50/30/20 budget.

    3.Track your progress

You can use a simple journal to do this. Also you can find apps under links on this page to automate tracking.

    4.Automate your savings

This simply means taking 20% of all your income and putting it directly towards savings or towards paying off debt. Remember to always start with the high interest debt first.

    5.Reacess budget

After a few months if you feel you have been making good progress paying down debt, then its time to reaccess. Similarly if you have a change in income you will need to reacess to stay ahead.
